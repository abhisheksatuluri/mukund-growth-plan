06, Psychological hooks

7 archetypes that stop the scroll.

The first 1.3 seconds decide whether his ICP, high-achievers 35-50, tunes in or thumb-scrolls past. Each archetype below has a mechanism (why it works), a formula, three examples written for Mukund's brand, and explicit guidance on when to use it. Built from the patterns Hormozi, Brunson, Welsh, and the top fitness creators (Nippard, Israetel, Syatt) systematically use to open content.

Deployment notes, these matter more than the hook itself

The 1.3-second test

Record the hook, mute the audio, watch only frames 0–40. If it doesn't communicate authority + tension visually in that window, the verbal hook won't save it. Mukund's physique + a London backdrop + an unexpected on-screen text caption is the visual hook stack.

Caption-hook discipline

~60% of Reels viewers in the 35–50 segment scroll muted (2025 Meta data). The caption is a second hook, not a recap. First line of caption should be a different archetype than the spoken one, addresses sound-off viewers.

Cadence rule

Rotate archetypes across any 7-post window. The algorithm signals diversity in opening patterns; viewer fatigue accelerates when one archetype dominates. Don't ship two False-Belief Kills back-to-back.

Hook A/B testing

Film 3 hooks per long-form. Cut each as a separate Reel from the same content. Whichever gets the strongest first-1-hour engagement wins, Meta surfaces it. The losing hooks become Stories (recovery use) or get archived.

What's been retired: "pattern_interrupt" (too generic) and "question_hook" (too easy to mentally answer + scroll). The 7 above absorb their function with sharper mechanisms, Question becomes Diagnostic Question with an unfindable benchmark; Pattern Interrupt becomes Authority Inversion with a credential anchor.

    Batch shoot day workflow

    Testimonial production guide

    Per Mukund's spec: filmed outside the gym for lifestyle feel, not inside it. The goal is to look like a documentary, not a sales testimonial. Target: 5–10 of these over the next 90 days.

    The 4-beat structure (every video)

    1. The struggle before. "Where were you when you first reached out? What had you tried? Why was it failing?", 20-25 sec.
    2. What changed. "What's different now? Physical, mental, behavioural. Be specific.", 25-30 sec. Real numbers (kg lost, energy, sleep, performance) beat adjectives.
    3. Why others should work with him. "If you were talking to someone exactly like you 12 months ago, what would you tell them?", 15-20 sec.
    4. The endorsement line. One closing sentence: "Best decision I made this year." / "I'd recommend Mukund to anyone serious about it." Cut as a standalone hero clip.

    Total run-time: 60-90 seconds per testimonial. Cut into a 30-sec social version + a 60-sec website version + a hero clip of the endorsement line.

    Setting + production

    • Outside the gym. Their office lobby, a London café terrace, the park where they run, their kitchen. Shows the life the result enables, not the place it was earned.
    • Mid-morning natural light. 10am-12pm best. Avoid harsh midday or magic-hour back-light (looks staged).
    • Two cameras if possible. Wide static + tighter handheld B-roll of them at the desk / walking / coffee. Cuts make it feel cinematic.
    • Lav mic always. Audio is the one thing you can't fix later.
    • Don't ask leading questions. Open-ended. Let the silence do the work, they'll fill it with the strongest line.
    • Capture the off-camera laugh. The unguarded moment is often the best 3 seconds in the whole edit.

    Compliance + permissions (UK ASA)

    • Written release before filming, explicit permission for: name, image, story, deployment across web, IG, YouTube, paid ads. Use a one-page template.
    • Flag claims you can't substantiate. "Lost 12kg in 16 weeks while travelling 3 weeks of it" is fine if it's true and documented. "Cured my back pain" is not, replace with "no back pain since" or similar.
    • Add the disclaimer line in caption: "Results vary. Outcomes shown reflect the individual client's adherence and starting point."

    09c, Lead capture pipeline

    Form → Sheet → WhatsApp ping.

    Every form submission on the site (audit completion + apply form) lands in one Google Sheet. Mukund gets a WhatsApp ping the moment a lead comes in. Free forever, no third-party SaaS in between.

    The architecture

    Step 1

    Site form (audit / apply / hero CTA) submits a JSON payload via fetch() to a Google Apps Script web-app endpoint.

    Step 2

    Apps Script appends a row to the Leads sheet with timestamp, source, name, email, score, tier interest, UTM tags, page URL, user agent.

    Step 3

    Apps Script fires a WhatsApp message via CallMeBot (free) to Mukund's personal number. Includes name, score, tier, time. He sees it in WhatsApp within seconds.

    Setup, step by step (one-time, ~15 min)

    1. Open the target sheet: the Leads tracking spreadsheet Abhishek shared.
    2. Extensions → Apps Script.
    3. Delete any default code. Paste the contents of scripts/lead_capture_apps_script.gs from the repo.
    4. Fill in the CONFIG block at the top with the CallMeBot apikey + Mukund's phone (steps to get the apikey are below).
    5. In the editor toolbar, run setupHeaders once. Authorise the script when Google asks.
    6. Deploy → New deployment → Web app. Execute as: Me. Who has access: Anyone. Click Deploy.
    7. Copy the Web app URL. It looks like https://script.google.com/macros/s/.../exec.
    8. Paste that URL into the website HTML, replacing __PASTE_GAS_WEB_APP_URL_HERE__ in:
      • Website/index.html (apply form)
      • Website/audit/index.html (audit email capture)
    9. Push, redeploy, test by submitting a form.

    CallMeBot WhatsApp setup (2 min, free)

    Note: CallMeBot rotates their bot WhatsApp number occasionally. If one says "Invite to WhatsApp" or doesn't reply within 2 minutes, try the next one.

    1. Save the current bot number to Mukund's phone contacts. Try in this order:
      • +34 623 78 64 49 (most recent, try first)
      • +34 698 28 89 73 (fallback)
      • +34 684 78 33 47 (fallback)
    2. Open WhatsApp chat with the saved contact and send: "I allow callmebot to send me messages" (exact text, lowercase).
    3. Wait up to 2 minutes for the bot's reply. It will contain his unique apikey.
    4. Paste the apikey into the GAS CONFIG block: 
      CALLMEBOT: {
  PHONE: '447943011882',     // his phone, no + or spaces
  APIKEY: 'XXXXXX',           // from the bot reply
}
    5. Run testNotify in the Apps Script editor. He should get a WhatsApp ping immediately.

    If all 3 numbers fail: CallMeBot may have rotated again. Check callmebot.com for the current number. Or switch the toggle in CONFIG to NOTIFY_TELEGRAM: true and follow the Telegram steps in the script header (more stable, official API).
