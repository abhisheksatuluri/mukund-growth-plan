06 — Psychological hooks

5 archetypes that stop the scroll.

The first 3 seconds decide whether his ICP — high-achieving men 35-50 — tunes in or scrolls past. These five archetypes are pulled from his proven winners.

Hook construction rules

  • First 3 seconds = visual + verbal hook simultaneously. Don't waste them on logos or intro.
  • Speak directly to the avatar. "If you're a London exec who..." beats "many people don't know that..."
  • Question hooks outperform statements — his #1 ever was a question ("Are you feeling stuck on your fat-loss journey?").
  • Numbers create specificity. "3 mistakes" beats "some mistakes" beats "common mistakes."
  • Negation creates intrigue. "I'm not training to look good. I'm training to live longer." (1,914 views)
  • Tease the payoff, deliver it within 30s on reels. Long-forms can hold longer — but never beyond 60s without paying off.
  • Avoid generic openers like "Today I'm going to talk about..." or "Here's what most people..." — algorithmic poison.
  • Test 3 hooks per video — film 3 versions of the first 5s, A/B test as separate reels (Meta will surface the winner).

Saves + shares from VERIFIED ICP accounts (founders / execs) signal Meta who his real audience is. One save from a Goldman director outweighs 100 likes from fitness peers.

Story polls — weekly demographic signal

Run one poll per week designed to make Meta map his audience to professional / executive demographics. Examples:

  • "What's your role? 👔 Founder/CEO   💼 Senior pro   🏃 Athlete   🎯 Other"
  • "Biggest training challenge? 🕐 No time   🛌 Recovery   🍽 Nutrition"
  • "Hours / week training? <3   3-5   6+"
  • "Are you 35+? Yes / No"

Meta uses poll responses to refine audience modelling. Within 8-12 weeks the algorithm learns "this creator's audience = 35+ professional men" — which is the segment we want.

    Why this lifts ICP fit (the algorithm logic)
