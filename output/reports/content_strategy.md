## YouTube long-form → Shorts repurposing

**Yes**: long-form YouTube should be the source. Mukund clearly speaks well (his 3-min "why" video, while format-wrong for IG, shows he has long-form chops). Recipe:

1. **One 12-20 min YouTube video / week** built around a single ICP question
2. **5-8 vertical clips cut from each** (using CapCut / Descript)
3. **Distribute** clips to IG Reels (Mon, Wed, Fri), TikTok (Tue, Thu, Sat), YouTube Shorts (continuous)
4. **Send the long-form to email list** as the weekly newsletter video

## CTA discipline

**Every reel ends with one of:**

- "DM **ELITE** to apply" (online)
- "Comment **AUDIT** to get the free scorecard" (lead magnet)
- "Apply via the link in bio" (high-intent)
- "DM **TEAM** if you run a London team" (corporate, LinkedIn-only)

If you can't pick one, the post is filler.

## Hooks by awareness stage

| Stage | Hook example |
|---|---|
| Unaware | "You are not lazy. Your plan is wrong." |
| Problem-aware | "Training 5 days but still no change?" |
| Solution-aware | "The 12-week structure I use with London execs." |
| Product-aware | "Apply for my 3-month transformation coaching." |
| Most-aware | "5 spaces open for May coaching cohort." |

Mukund's corpus skews heavily to *solution-aware* and *unaware* — the missing layer is **problem-aware** (the highest-converting layer for cold traffic). That's why the "fat loss stuck?" reel hit 6,746 views while mindset reels hit 81.

## Production guardrails

- **Phone-shot is fine.** Best-performing reels are not high-production.
- **Setting**: gym + outdoor + occasional desk/talking-head (LinkedIn).
- **Length**: 15-45s for IG Reels; 60-90s for YouTube Shorts; 12-20 min for YouTube long-form.
- **Subtitles**: always (his current videos already do this — keep it).
- **Don't**: 3-minute talking-head reels; "deep mindset" without proof.

## Risk / compliance flags

- "**Sick people are profitable**" (DUEIqPdjHrL) — borderline conspiracy framing. ASA wouldn't love it; corporate buyers definitely won't. Rewrite or retire.
- Nutrition reels ("ultra-processed food keeps you addicted") need disclaimer wording.
- Before/after transformation imagery (referenced in DHTMj_-osHm) needs ASA-compliant captioning ("typical results may vary; testimonials reflect participation in 12-week programme").
